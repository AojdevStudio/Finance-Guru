## Appendix A: Glossary

**Return of Capital (ROC)**: A distribution that returns your own invested capital to you. Not taxable when received, but reduces your cost basis. When you sell, you'll have a larger capital gain.

**Qualified Dividends**: Dividends that meet IRS requirements and are taxed at favorable long-term capital gains rates (0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income).

**Ordinary Income**: Income taxed at your marginal tax rate (10-37%). Most options premium income is taxed as ordinary income.

**Premium/Discount to NAV**: For closed-end funds (CEFs), the difference between market price and the actual value of the underlying holdings. A 20% premium means you're paying $1.20 for $1.00 of assets.

**30-Day SEC Yield**: Standardized yield calculation that excludes return of capital. This is the "true" sustainable yield of a fund.

**VIX (Volatility Index)**: Measure of expected market volatility. Higher VIX = higher option premiums = higher covered call income.

**Covered Call**: Option strategy where you own stock and sell call options against it. Generates premium income but caps upside potential.
